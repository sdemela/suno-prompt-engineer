Suno Style Prompt Cheat Sheet — 50+ Keywords That Work

March 2026 · Quick reference · Updated for Suno v4 and v5

This is the quick reference you come back to when writing Suno style prompts. Every keyword here has been tested across multiple Suno versions and produces consistent, useful results. Organized by category so you can mix and match to build your own prompts.

Use these as building blocks. A good Suno style prompt typically picks 3–6 keywords from different categories and arranges them in order: genre → energy → instruments → production texture → vocals.

How to use this list

Pick one or two from each relevant category. Start with genre, then energy, then instruments and production. For Suno v5 you can combine keywords into short descriptive phrases. For v3 and v4, keep them as individual keywords separated by commas.

Genre & Subgenre

The foundation of every prompt. Be specific — "tech house" works better than "electronic", and "dark R&B" works better than "R&B".

Instruments & Sound Sources

Specific instrument names outperform generic categories. "TR-909 kick" tells Suno more than "electronic drums". "upright bass" tells it more than "bass".

Space and reverb character dramatically affect the feel of a track. Use these to control how "big" or "intimate" the sound is.

Build your own

A solid prompt formula: [genre] + [energy] + [1–2 instruments] + [production texture] + [reverb] + [vocal direction]. That's all you need for a reliable starting point. Then iterate from there.

Keywords to Avoid

These either violate Suno's terms of service or simply don't produce reliable results:
